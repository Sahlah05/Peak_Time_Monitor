====================================================================
STAGE 2: SYNTHESIS (CADENCE GENUS)
====================================================================
This folder contains:
- Timing, Power, Gate Count, and Area Reports
- Synthesis Output Screenshot

Tools Used:
- Cadence Genus Synthesis Solution

Workflow:
1. Set up the SCL180 standard cell and I/O pad liberty libraries
   (slow/fast corners) for timing and power characterization.
2. Read in the RTL (ptm_main.v, ptm.v, clk_div.v) and elaborated the
   design, running check_design to confirm a clean, unresolved-free
   netlist.
3. Applied SDC constraints: a 30 ns clock period on ptm_clk, setup/
   hold clock uncertainty, input/output delays relative to the clock,
   an output load of 0.05 pF, and false-path/path-grouping exceptions
   for the asynchronous reset.
4. Ran the three-stage synthesis flow -- generic mapping, technology
   mapping, and incremental optimization -- generating the mapped
   Verilog netlist and updated SDC at each stage.
5. Generated Logical Equivalence Check (LEC) scripts to verify the
   mapped netlist against the RTL golden model.
6. Generated PPA (Power, Performance, Area) sign-off reports and an
   SDF file for back-annotated timing simulation.

Learning Outcomes:
- Learned how SDC constraints (clock definition, I/O delays,
  uncertainty, false paths) directly shape the synthesis tool's
  optimization targets.
- Understood the generic -> map -> incremental optimization flow and
  why reports are captured at each stage to track QoR (Quality of
  Results) progression.
- Learned how to interpret timing, power, gate count, and area
  reports to sanity-check a synthesized netlist before moving to
  physical design.
