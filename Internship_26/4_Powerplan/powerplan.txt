STAGE 4: POWER PLANNING (CADENCE INNOVUS)
====================================================================
This folder contains:
- VDD/VSS Power Ring and Stripe Specifications
- Power Grid Verification Reports
- Power Plan Layout Screenshot

Tools Used:
- Cadence Innovus

Workflow:
1. Connected global power/ground pins (VDD, VSS, VDDO, VSSO) and
   tie-high/tie-low nets across every instance in the design using
   globalNetConnect.
2. Created core power rings for VDD_CORE and VSS_CORE around the core
   boundary on the M5/TOP_M metal layers, 25 micron wide with 25
   micron spacing and a 1.8 micron offset from the core edge.
3. Added vertical power stripes on the TOP_M layer (10 micron wide,
   10 micron spacing, 100 micron set-to-set distance) to distribute
   supply current evenly across the core.
4. Routed the power rings/stripes down to standard-cell power pins
   using sroute, allowing layer changes between M1 and TOP_M.
5. Verified connectivity, ran a full design check, and re-checked
   pre-placement timing on the powered floorplan.

Learning Outcomes:
- Mastered structural power planning (rings + stripes) to ensure
  uniform voltage delivery across the core.
- Learned how robust power grids prevent local IR voltage drops
  across a running chip.
- Understood how sroute connects the global power mesh down to
  individual standard-cell power pins.
